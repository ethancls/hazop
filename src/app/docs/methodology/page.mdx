
# Methodology

HAZOP Labs follows the standard HAZOP methodology widely used in the chemical process industry.

## Principles

The core concept is to systematically identify potential hazards by brainstorming deviations from the design intent.

### 1. Nodes & Design Intent
The process is divided into **nodes** (e.g., specific pipes, vessels, or reactors). For each node, the **design intent** (expected operation) is clearly defined.

### 2. Parameters
Physical or chemical properties of the process:
- Flow
- Pressure
- Temperature
- Level
- Composition
- pH
- Viscosity

### 3. Guide Words
Standard words applied to parameters to generate **deviations**:
- **NO / NONE**: No flow, no pressure (complete negation)
- **MORE**: Higher flow, higher temperature (quantitative increase)
- **LESS**: Lower flow, lower pressure (quantitative decrease)
- **AS WELL AS**: Impurities present (qualitative increase)
- **PART OF**: Missing components (qualitative decrease)
- **REVERSE**: Reverse flow (logical opposite)
- **OTHER THAN**: Maintenance, startup, shutdown (complete substitution)

### 4. Risk Ranking
Risks are evaluated based on a **Risk Matrix**:
- **Severity**: Impact on safety, environment, or assets (1-5).
- **Likelihood**: Probability of the event occurring (1-5).
- **Risk Level**: Derived from Severity x Likelihood (Low, Medium, High, Critical).
